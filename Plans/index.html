North London Coffee Co. — Click-through Prototype

A walk-through of the three core workflows for the v1 product: receive green, plan & run a roast, and fulfil an order into the EU. The product is metric by default, EUR/GBP-native, and EUDR-ready — every screen reflects the design in the v1.1 PRD. This is a wireframe, not a pixel-perfect design; the goal is to test whether each step makes sense to a real UK/EU roaster, not to show what the colours will be.

Persona: North London Coffee Co., 9 staff, ~3,500 kg/mo, multi-channel (DTC + 12 cafés + 1 private label)
Region: United Kingdom, data residency uk, base currency GBP
User in this walkthrough: Aisha (head roaster + acting compliance officer), with the buyer Sam adding a new green lot

Why all this for one lot?

From 30 December 2026, every shipment of coffee into the EU must be backed by a Due Diligence Statement with a producer geolocation, supplier risk review, and a chain of custody. Capturing it once at receipt means the system generates the DDS in under two minutes at ship time, not a 90-minute scramble.

You can still receive this lot

Receiving this lot is allowed because the destination risk is the issue, not the receipt. The system has three possible paths to an EU shipment from this lot:

  1. Complete the supplier risk assessment (10 minutes) — recommended. This unblocks EU shipment for any amount of this lot without further work.
  2. Ship to the EU and file a DDS — works once the supplier risk assessment is in place. Standard path.
  3. Ship to the EU without a DDS — only via the per-shipment opt-out path (see screen 3.4a). The system records your decision, your reason, and your role. The opt-out is *not* available for lots from EU high-risk countries — this one isn't, so it's available.
Receive 240 kg anyway Complete supplier onboarding now (10 min) Save lot as draft
Why we don't block by default. Many UK-UK roasters receive lots they have no intention of selling to the EU. Forcing every receiving to do EUDR paperwork would push them off the product. The system warns here and locks down at the moment of an EU shipment — that's when the legal exposure actually starts.

What you can do to unblock

  1. Complete the supplier risk assessment for Origin & Bean Co. (10 min) — unblocks this shipment and all future EU shipments from this lot. Start onboarding
  2. Choose a different lot for this shipment. Reallocate the Yirgacheffe portion of WS-2026-0421 to NLCC-2026-0418-KEN-AA (Kenya AA, fully assessed). Reallocate
  3. Take the per-shipment opt-out for this lot only. Recorded, authorised, audit-trail generated. The opt-out is not available for lots from EU high-risk countries — this supplier isn't, so the opt-out is available. Go to opt-out screen
  4. Hold the shipment. The customer will be notified of a delay. Hold & notify customer
Why this is a hard block, not a warning. The receiving moment (screen 1.3) was a warning because no EU exposure existed. The shipment moment is a block because EU exposure exists and the underlying data isn't there. The two moments have different rules on purpose.

This shipment is in scope for EUDR. Destination: Berlin, DE. Net mass: 14.36 kg (above 1 kg threshold). One of the green lots (NLCC-2026-0404-ETH-YG-AB) doesn't have a current risk assessment on file, so the standard DDS path is blocked.

What you're about to do

To ship this without a Due Diligence Statement, we need you to confirm a few things. This will create a permanent, signed record in your audit log.

If a regulator later asks why this shipment did not have a DDS, you will be able to show them this record: the signed opt-out audit pack, with the reason, the role, the timestamp, and the chain of custody.

1. Why is this shipment not getting a DDS? *

421 / 500 characters

2. Who is making this decision?

Aisha (you) — head_roaster ✓

Your role qualifies for this decision. packer and accountant roles cannot take this action. readonly cannot either. The decision is attributed to you by name and role in the audit pack.

3. Confirm: type the phrase below exactly *

You must type this phrase. The system is matching your input character-by-character against the required text. The phrase is the legal acknowledgement that goes into the audit pack.

I confirm this shipment does not require a Due Diligence Statement under EUDR. I am the operator of record.
✓ Matches required phrase

What happens after you confirm

  1. The system records a ShipmentEudrDecision with your reason, your role, and the timestamp.
  2. A signed opt-out audit pack is generated (2-3 page PDF).
  3. An entry is written to the tamper-evident audit log.
  4. The shipment unblocks; the DPD label can print; the box can leave.
  5. Father & Sons get a read-only link to the audit pack on the wholesale portal (if you choose to share).
You can still change your mind. If you later decide to file a DDS for this shipment anyway, the system creates a new decision linking to this one. The original opt-out record is preserved; the new one supersedes it.
Why the friction is here. The system has to make this decision *deliberate* so that a regulator, a year from now, can see exactly who decided what and why. The friction isn't user-hostile — it's the difference between "we have a record" and "we don't know this happened."

What the audit pack contains (for a regulator)

  1. Cover page: org name, shipment ID, destination, date, the legal acknowledgement text, the signature, a verification QR code.
  2. Lot genealogy: the chain from the green lot(s) through the roast, the packaged lot, the order line, to the shipment.
  3. Reason: the reason code and the free-text reason as recorded.
  4. User record: who decided, what role, when.
  5. Hash chain: the prior audit-log entry's hash and the new entry's hash, demonstrating the chain of custody.
